## ğŸ”¥ Fire: A Double-Edged Sword ğŸ”¥

For the Fynbos, fire isn't just a destructive force; it's a lifeline. Fire stimulates these plants, urging them to release their seeds and reproduce. But there's a catch! These species require fire at specific moments in their life cycle. Too soon, and the Fynbos can't germinate properly. Too late, and invasive species take over, pushing out the native Fynbos.

The consequences? A drastic reduction in local biodiversity and a heightened risk of extreme fires fueled by the more flammable invasive species. These frequent fires pose a grave threat to both animals and humans, leading to long-lasting negative impacts.
